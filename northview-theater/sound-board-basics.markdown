# Sound Board Basics: What Does the Mixer Do?

[Back to Sound Crew Hub](/northview-theater/) | [Troubleshooting](/northview-theater/troubleshooting/)

---

The sound board (also called the **mixer**) is the heart of the audio system. It lets us control the volume, tone, and routing of all the microphones and music during a show.

This guide explains the basics of the mixer for anyone helping with sound - whether you're a middle school student running their first show, or an adult director pitching in during tech week.

---

## The Big Idea

The mixer takes sound from many sources (like mics and music) and sends it out to different places (like the main speakers, monitors, and headphones). You can:

- Turn individual sounds **up or down**
- Mute a mic if someone isn't speaking
- Adjust **tone** with EQ
- Apply effects (like reverb or compression)
- Control **who hears what**

---

## Key Parts of the Mixer

| Part | What It Does |
|------|--------------|
| **Channel Fader** | Controls the volume of one mic or input |
| **Mute Button** | Turns a channel off without moving the fader |
| **Select Button** | Lets you change EQ and other settings for that channel |
| **Gain (Trim)** | Controls how much signal is allowed in from the mic |
| **Pan** | Sends the sound more to the left or right speaker |
| **Main Fader** | Controls the overall output volume to the audience |
| **Solo** | Sends the sound to headphones without affecting the audience |

---

## What You Should Do

- Keep all **vocal mics labeled** clearly (e.g., "Mic 1 - Jamie")
- Keep **faders around 0 (unity)** for normal use
- Use **Mute** when someone isn't speaking
- Check that **Main L/R is turned on** for each channel
- If something sounds wrong, **ask before changing too much**

---

## What Not to Do

| DON'T | Why |
|-------|-----|
| Don't touch EQ or gain during a show | Small changes can make big problems |
| Don't mute or solo channels randomly | It can cut out important mics |
| Don't push faders past the top line | That can cause distortion or feedback |
| Don't turn off the mixer during a show | It shuts down the whole sound system! |

---

## For New Crew Members

- Always **check with the sound lead** before changing settings
- If you're not sure what a button does, it's okay to ask!
- Try practicing with **just one mic** and learning what each control does
