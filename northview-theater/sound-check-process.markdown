# Sound Check Process (Theater Style)

[Back to Sound Crew Hub](/northview-theater/) | [Troubleshooting](/northview-theater/troubleshooting/)

---

A sound check helps us make sure every mic and audio source is working, clear, and loud enough - **before** actors hit the stage. It's especially important in musical theater, where performers speak and sing with **varying volume and emotion**, and we ride mic faders live to keep everything balanced and feedback-free.

This guide outlines our **fader-down sound check process**, based on how many professional theater board operators work - and how we run things here.

---

## When Does Sound Check Happen?

- At the start of **each dress rehearsal and show day**
- Before the **audience arrives**
- Usually takes **10-20 minutes** if everyone is ready to go

---

## What We're Trying to Do

- Make sure **each mic is transmitting clearly**
- Set **input gain** so faders don't have to be pushed too far during the show
- Check for **feedback, distortion, or dropouts**
- Let actors get comfortable hearing themselves
- Give the board operator a feel for **real-world cue mixing**

---

## Before You Begin

### Sound Crew:
- Power on the mixer, wireless receivers, and any playback equipment
- Verify all mic packs have **fresh batteries**
- Ensure channels are **labeled by actor and mic number**
- Confirm the correct mic is assigned to the correct channel

### Cast:
- Wear mics as they will be worn during the show
- Stay quiet until your mic is called
- Speak **in character** when testing - not just "check 1-2-3"

---

## Sound Check Process

### 1. Test One Actor at a Time (Fader-Down Style)

1. **Unmute** the actor's channel, but keep the **fader all the way down** (-infinity)
2. Ask the actor to say a line in character and volume
3. **Bring the fader up slowly**, just as you would during the show
4. Adjust the **input gain (trim)** so the fader lands comfortably between **-10 and 0 dB** when they speak clearly
5. Listen for:
   - Clarity? Distortion? Dropouts?
   - Boomy, muffled, or nasal sound?
   - Any hint of feedback?
6. If needed, apply light EQ (high-pass filter, low-mid cut, high-mid clarity boost)
7. Return the **fader to -infinity**, leave the mic **muted until showtime**
8. Move on to the next actor

---

### 2. Group Mic Check (Optional)

- With multiple mics live, test a group scene or choral number
- This helps catch **feedback problems**, mic bleed, or level imbalances
- Practice **fader rides** for group cueing

---

### 3. Music Cue Check

- Play a short music cue or sound effect
- Confirm:
  - Correct playback input (Aux or Tape In)
  - Good level balance with vocals
  - No distortion or hum

---

## Troubleshooting During Sound Check

| Problem | What to Check |
|---------|---------------|
| No sound from a mic | Is the pack on? Batteries OK? Receiver linked? |
| Mic too quiet | Raise **input gain**, not the fader alone |
| Mic too loud/distorted | Lower **input gain** first |
| Feedback starts early | Reduce gain, adjust EQ, check mic placement |
| Static/dropouts | Check mic cable, antenna visibility, or battery |

---

## DOs and DON'Ts

| DO | DON'T |
|----|-------|
| Leave faders down and test with real movement | Don't check mics with faders left at unity |
| Speak in character - not just "test" | Don't test all mics at once |
| Keep show fader movements consistent | Don't over-adjust EQ without reason |
| Give every actor their own focused check | Don't skip ensemble mics "just for tonight" |

---

## For New Crew Members

- **Only one mic live at a time** during testing - this keeps feedback risks low
- Always return faders to -infinity after checking
- Listen for **natural sound** - like the actor is right in front of you
- If a mic sounds bad, it's okay to ask for help - don't fake your way through it
