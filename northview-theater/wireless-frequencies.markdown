# Wireless Frequency Management

[Back to Sound Crew Hub](/northview-theater/) | [Troubleshooting](/northview-theater/troubleshooting/)

---

Our Sennheiser EW-D receivers are pre-configured to specific frequencies. **These settings should not be changed unless working with the technical director.**

Our Audio-Technica ATW-3100b receivers *might* be configured to specific frequencies. **These should be double checked during tech week of each performance.**

There are two additional ATW-R3100b(C) receivers under the desk/lightboard that are **not currently in use**:

---

## Why Frequency Matters

- **Spacing prevents interference**: These frequencies are intentionally spaced apart so the mics don't interfere with each other
- **Body pack compatibility**: Only body packs on the same frequency will work with their matching receiver
- **Pre-configured = reliable**: The frequencies have been chosen to avoid common sources of interference

---

## For Sound Board Operators

When checking receivers before the show:

- Verify each receiver shows the correct frequency listed above
- Check that each receiver is receiving signal from its body pack
- If you see frequency errors or "No Link" warnings, tell the technical director - don't try to fix it yourself

---

## Advanced Settings (Technical Director Reference Only)

Our receivers have several settings optimized for theater use:

- **High Pass Filter**: Enabled on all channels to reduce feedback by eliminating low frequencies below what mics typically capture
- **Squelch settings**: Adjusted to minimize background interference
- **Gain structure**: Set to work optimally with our mixer input levels

**For interested crew members**: If you want to learn more about how the wireless system works, ask to see the ATW-R3100b receiver manual. Understanding the system can help with troubleshooting, but these settings should only be adjusted by the technical director.

---

## For New Crew Members

- You don't need to memorize these frequencies - just know that each mic has a specific receiver it must use
- If a receiver shows "No Link" or the wrong frequency, that's a technical problem - get help immediately
- The pre-configured frequencies are one of the reasons our setup usually "just works"
