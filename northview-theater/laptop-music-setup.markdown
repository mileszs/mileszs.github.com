# Setting Up a Laptop for Music Cues

[Back to Sound Crew Hub](/northview-theater/) | [Troubleshooting](/northview-theater/troubleshooting/)

---

Whether we're using professional cueing software like **QLab**, a **publisher-provided app**, or just **VLC**, the way we set up the laptop for music playback can make or break the show.

This guide explains how to connect the laptop to the sound board, organize and test the music files, and avoid common mistakes that can disrupt a performance.

---

## What You *Should* Do

### 1. Use the AUX IN on the Sound Board

- The **AUX IN** (Aux A/B) on the sound board is designed for music sources like laptops
- It expects the right kind of signal (called *line level*) and gives us full control over volume and EQ

> **Use a 3.5mm headphone plug to dual 1/4" TS cable.** The small end goes into the laptop's headphone jack, and the larger plugs go into **Aux A and B** on the board.

---

### 2. Set the Laptop Volume Around 75-90%

- Don't max out the laptop volume - it may cause distortion
- Don't leave it too quiet - it can introduce hiss or weak sound
- Adjust balance using the mixer's fader, not the laptop

---

### 3. Use Good Cueing Software

- **QLab** (Mac only) is ideal for timed music and sound cues
- Some shows come with their own cueing apps - test them ahead of time
- **VLC** works in a pinch, but it requires more manual control

---

### 4. Test the Loudest Cue

- Play the loudest part of a music track while setting levels
- This ensures you won't get unexpected peaks or feedback during a big moment

---

### 5. Label the Laptop Input Clearly

- On the board or in the scene file, label the channel as **"Laptop"**, **"Music"**, or **"Playback"**
- Color-code the fader strip if needed

---

## File Storage & Playback Tips

### Always Copy Tracks to the Computer's Internal Drive

- USB thumb drives may **disconnect** or **go to sleep** when the laptop is idle or closed
- When this happens, **VLC or QLab may lose track of your music files** - cue lists may reset, files may go missing, or order may be lost
- Save your tracks in a dedicated folder like `SpringShow_Cues` directly on the computer's desktop or documents folder

### Keep the USB Drive as a Backup

- Bring a second copy of your cues on a USB drive or external hard drive
- Label it clearly: **"Backup Cues - DO NOT REMOVE"**
- This gives you a fallback if something happens to the laptop or primary files

---

## Playback Software Tips

### VLC
- Use **playlist view** and test that all tracks play in the correct order
- Set **"Stop after each track"** if your version allows it (to avoid autoplay mishaps)
- Don't close the laptop lid or let it sleep - it may disconnect the USB or reset VLC's memory
- Reopen and check the cue list before the show begins

### QLab
- Save your **workspace file** and name cues clearly (e.g., "Scene 5 Underscore")
- Use "**Build workspace**" to bundle media into the file (Mac only)
- Run a quick rehearsal of **all cues in order** before the house opens

### Publisher Apps
- These vary! If your show comes with official playback software:
  - Test it on the actual laptop you'll use for the show
  - Avoid updating the app or OS the day of the show
  - If it needs internet access, make sure the venue has stable Wi-Fi

---

## What *Not* to Do

| DON'T | Why |
|-------|-----|
| Don't use a mic input | It's too sensitive and causes distortion |
| Don't max out laptop volume | It can overload the mixer and clip the signal |
| Don't rely on Bluetooth | Bluetooth adds delay and can randomly disconnect |
| Don't run playback from USB alone | If disconnected, your tracks may vanish mid-show |

---

## For New Crew Members

- Always check the playlist **before the audience enters**
- Don't assume it's saved - click through every cue manually to be sure
- Keep your laptop **plugged in**, **notifications off**, and **sleep disabled** during the show
- Have a backup ready - even a phone with the cues and the right cable is better than silence
