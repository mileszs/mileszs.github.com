# Mic Pack Battery & Labeling Guidelines

[Back to Sound Crew Hub](/northview-theater/) | [Troubleshooting](/northview-theater/troubleshooting/)

---

A mic pack that dies during a show is one of the most stressful problems for sound crew and actors. Good **battery habits** and **clear labeling** help everything run smoothly and prevent awkward silent solos on stage.

---

## What Is a Mic Pack?

A **mic pack** is the small wireless transmitter that sends an actor's voice to the sound board. It clips to a waistband or costume and connects to a headset mic. If the **battery runs out** or the **pack gets mixed up**, that actor's mic will stop working - even if it's placed perfectly!

---

## Battery Guidelines

### 1. What Kind of Batteries Do We Use?

| Wireless System | Battery Type |
|-----------------|--------------|
| Sennheiser EW-D | **AA batteries only** (currently) |
| Audio-Technica ATW-3100b | **AA batteries only** |

We use **standard AA batteries** in all packs for now.

> **Warning:** Do not use cheap dollar-store batteries - they often don't last through a full show.

---

### 2. Battery Replacement Rules

| Situation | What to Do |
|-----------|------------|
| **Before each full show** | Use fresh batteries in every pack. No exceptions. |
| **During rehearsals** | Replace only if the battery is below half or running low |
| **Long tech days (4+ hrs)** | Swap batteries halfway through if unsure |

> **Important:** Never reuse batteries across multiple shows unless you're absolutely sure they're full.

---

### 3. Can We Use Rechargeable Batteries?

Right now, **we use disposable AAs**, but here's what's possible:

**Sennheiser EW-D:**
These packs **can safely use rechargeable AAs**, and there's even an **official rechargeable battery module (BA 70)** available. If we invest in that:
- We get **accurate percentage-based battery readouts**
- Packs can be **charged in a dock overnight** - no battery swaps needed
- More eco-friendly and potentially cost-saving long-term

**Audio-Technica ATW-3100b:**
These do **not work well with rechargeable batteries**:
- Battery life indicators are less accurate
- Rechargeables may cause **sudden shutdowns** or **dropouts**
- Stick with **disposable AAs** for reliability in live shows

---

### 4. Battery Handling Tips

- Store spares in a **labeled, dry container** backstage
- Don't mix old and new batteries in a pack
- Recycle or dispose of used batteries properly - keep a **used battery box** in the booth or backstage

---

## Labeling Guidelines

### 1. Label Each Mic Pack by Actor Name or Number

- Use **masking tape** or small printed labels
- Label the **mic pack**, **headset**, and **receiver channel** to match

> **Example:** "Mic 4 - Max" on the pack, headset, and sound board

---

### 2. Use Color-Coding (Optional)

| Color | Mic # | Notes |
|-------|-------|-------|
| Blue Tape | Mic 1 | Lead Male |
| Red Tape | Mic 2 | Lead Female |
| Yellow | Mic 3 | Ensemble |

Use colors along with names - helps with fast mic swaps during a show.

---

### 3. Mic Assignment Chart

Make a simple table showing:
- Actor -> Mic Number -> Mixer Channel

Keep a copy:
- At the **sound board**
- **Backstage near mic wrangling area**
- In the **cue binder or tech script**

---

## For New Crew Members

- Always check the battery level before handing out a mic
- Ask before changing brands or trying rechargeable batteries
- If a mic dies during the show, swap to a labeled **backup pack** - don't try to change batteries mid-scene

---

## Emergency Backup Plan

In an ideal world, we would be prepared to have this emergency backup plan. We can't quite pull this off at the moment. Consider this aspirational.

- Keep 1-2 **fully set up spare packs** (with fresh batteries and labeled headsets) backstage
- If a mic fails, swap the actor into a backup pack quickly and inform the sound board operator
