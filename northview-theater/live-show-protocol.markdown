# Live Show Protocol for Sound Crew

[Back to Sound Crew Hub](/northview-theater/) | [Troubleshooting](/northview-theater/troubleshooting/)

---

The goal during a live performance is simple: **Make the cast sound great** and **Prevent anything from breaking or feeding back.**

This guide explains the roles, expectations, and rhythm of running sound during a show - whether you're at the board or backstage helping with mics.

---

## 1. Pre-Show Checklist (60-30 Minutes Before Curtain)

### Sound Board Operator:

For the cafetorium, use the [Mobile Sound Setup Checklist](https://docs.google.com/document/d/122Vr53L-1aY62hv7na49Zpc0uln7LtZHipkKGeNX-sk/edit?usp=drive_link)

For the old Northview theater:
- Turn on the mixer and all wireless receivers
- Turn on the main speakers and monitors
- Verify all channels are correctly labeled and assigned
- Load the correct **scene or preset** if one is saved
- Check that music or sound effect sources (QLab, laptop, etc.) are connected and ready
- Confirm the **Main L/R mix is routed and active**

### Backstage/Assistant Crew:

- Hand out mics to actors according to the mic chart
- Help with mic placement (see [Mic Placement Guide](/northview-theater/mic-placement/))
- Check that each pack is:
  - **On**
  - **Showing signal**
  - **Not muted**
  - **Securely attached**
- Run a **quick pack tap test** or speak test if time allows

---

## 2. Final Sound Check (15-10 Minutes Before Curtain)

- One last check to ensure:
  - No feedback
  - All actors have their correct mics
  - Music playback is working
- **Keep all faders at -infinity** until needed - this prevents accidental open mics

> **Tip:** Some shows benefit from a 2-3 minute "house music off / quiet test" with the actors already mic'd - it helps spot last-minute issues before the audience hears them.

---

## 3. During the Show

### Sound Board Operator (A1):

- Follow the script or cue sheet carefully
- Keep **faders down until just before the actor speaks**
- Use **input gain + fader** to balance each voice - adjust live for shouting or whispering
- **Never leave a mic open** when the actor isn't speaking - prevents feedback and noise

**Special Notes:**
- Watch for mic swaps if an actor's pack fails (backups should be ready)
- If feedback starts:
  1. Lower the fader
  2. Look at actor's position (in front of speaker?)
  3. Apply a quick EQ cut or pan if needed

### Backstage Sound Crew:

- Be ready to:
  - **Swap batteries or full packs** mid-show
  - **Adjust or re-tape** mics that slip or fall off
  - **Run interference** if an actor is missing their mic call

---

## 4. Emergency Protocols

| Situation | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| Mic dies mid-scene | Hand actor a pre-labeled backup pack if possible |
| Feedback starts | Drop the fader and mute the mic immediately |
| Music or cue playback fails | Switch to manual backup (e.g. play from phone or USB) |

> **Pro tip:** Keep a small sound emergency kit backstage: fresh batteries, pre-labeled backup mic pack, spare tape, and windscreen.

---

## 5. Post-Show Wrap-Up

- Turn off all wireless packs and receivers
- Collect all mics and store neatly (untape carefully!)
- Check for damage or broken tape connections
- Recharge or replace batteries as needed for the next performance
- Save any mix changes that worked well during the show

> Clean windscreens and mic booms gently if multiple actors use the same headset.

---

## DOs and DON'Ts

| DO | DON'T |
|----|-------|
| Stay focused and follow the script | Don't chat or check your phone during cues |
| Keep your finger on the mute if needed | Don't leave faders up "just in case" |
| Communicate quietly and clearly backstage | Don't yell for someone to "fix the mic!" |
| Stay calm and professional | Don't panic if something breaks - work the plan |

---

## For New Crew Members

- Learn mic cue timing by watching rehearsals closely
- Always know which mics are "live" at any moment
- If something goes wrong, **lower the fader first**, then troubleshoot
- Watch the A1 - ask questions during tech, not the show
